This is Qian

Design Researcher / Designer

I'm a PhD researcher/designer at the Human-Computer Interaction Institute at Carnegie Mellon University. I'm co-advised by Dr. John Zimmerman and Dr. Aaron Steinfeld. My research inverstigates Design in HCI practices, with a focus on how design can make data science better support human-centered decision-making process.
My two ongoing projects are:

CORA: Design a Medical Decision Support Tool

I am designing a decisions support tool that mines medical histories and makes an intervention recommendation to advanced heart failure patients and physicians.
My research and design raise questions from a human-factor perspective:
How can we make the computational tool better merged into a human-centered decision-making process?
How can we design data mining outputs in a way that can lead to better decision-making process and thus better medical decision?
What is a better decision-making process? What is a better end-of-life decision anyway?

Tiramisu: Crowd-sourced Public Transit

The Tiramisu project investigates how social media can provide new ways for citizens to take ownership of and responsibility for their public services. As a former car designer, I'm highly excited about the new design possibilities enabled by personal transit prediction and other crowd-sourced data.

Before coming to CMU...

I worked as a design trend researcher and consultant for more than 5 years in Shanghai. My researches highlight Chinese customer aesthetic, attitudinal and behavioral insights, and help companies from multiple industries including automotive, FMCG, and technology understand their users and localize their products from early stages of product development.
